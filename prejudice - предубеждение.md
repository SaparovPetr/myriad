# prejudice - предубеждение

1. to have a prejudice
2. to face prejudice
3. racial prejudice
4. deep-seated prejudice
5. without prejudice
6. prejudice against
7. overcome prejudice

- She has always had a prejudice against fast food, believing it is unhealthy.
- He faced considerable prejudice when he moved to the small, insular town.
- The company's policy aims to eliminate racial prejudice in the hiring process.
- For many years, a deep-seated prejudice prevented them from accepting the change.
- The offer was made without prejudice, allowing further negotiation.
- There is still a lot of prejudice against people from different backgrounds.
- They worked hard to overcome the prejudice in their community.

Present Perfect: The community has overcome much of its historical prejudice.
Past Simple: He faced prejudice throughout his early career.
Past Continuous: She was constantly facing prejudice because of her accent.
Past Perfect: They had harbored a prejudice long before they met him.
